ABOUT

Fake Indian Currency Note (FICN) is the official term for counterfeit notes in the Indian economy. Estimates vary as to how many fake notes are in circulation. A study completed by the National Investigation Agency in partnership with the Indian Statistical Institute (ISI) stated that the value is 400 crore rupees (4 billion rupees/$53.3 million).



Counterfeit Indian currency is a pressing issue, primarily circulating in denominations of 500 and 2,000 rupees. These fake notes often enter circulation through illegal channels, posing a threat to the economy and financial stability. Sophisticated printing techniques, including high-quality paper and color replication, make detection challenging. Security features like watermarks, security threads, and holograms on genuine currency are mimicked by counterfeiters. Law enforcement agencies employ advanced technology and public awareness campaigns to combat this menace, emphasizing vigilance in financial transactions. Continuous efforts are underway to enhance currency security and implement stricter measures against the production and circulation of fake Indian currency.
